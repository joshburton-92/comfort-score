Hard fail rules
These rules eliminate locations that fail a threshold outright (score zero for that criterion). Toggle off to see how they'd rank without the rule.
Humidity hard fail (peak dew point >21°C = zero)
Eliminates tropical lowlands where the air feels oppressively muggy. Turn off if you're comfortable in humid heat — SE Asia, coastal Brazil, Caribbean.
Tropical winter fail (no cold season = zero)
Eliminates locations where the coldest month's average maximum stays ≥20°C — no real winter at all. Turn off if you prefer year-round warmth with no cold season.
Severe cold fail (coldest month mean ≤−2°C = zero)
Eliminates locations with genuinely harsh winters. Turn off if you love cold climates or winter sports destinations.
How it works
This survey evaluates year-round human liveability — not just pleasant summers or mild winters, but the complete annual experience of living somewhere. It deliberately rewards balance. SCORING CRITERIA (max 65 points) 1. Summer comfort — hottest month avg max (10pts) 2. Winter comfort — coldest month mean temp (10pts) 3. Rainfall — annual total (10pts) 4. Humidity — peak monthly dew point (10pts) 5. Sunshine — annual hours (10pts) 6. Snowfall — annual total (10pts) 7. Wind — daily average speed (5pts) QUALIFIERS score in all criteria with no hard fails. Marginals score 1/10 on one criterion. Fails score zero on at least one. YOUR PREFERENCES The preference sliders let you shift the scoring thresholds to match your personal climate taste. Summer and winter ideal temperatures, rainfall preference, sunshine threshold, snowfall preference, and hard-fail rules are all adjustable. Locations are re-scored in real time. Note: Only Australia and New Zealand currently have full raw climate data for preference re-scoring. More regions coming soon. DATA All scores based on long-term climate averages from official meteorological agencies — BOM (Australia), NIWA (New Zealand), WMO normals, NOAA, and regional met services.
